Daisy Mae and Rosebud

“In a ditch, alongside a country road, wildflowers and other plants grew. In that ditch, grasses, lilies, Queen Anne’s Lace, clovers, and milkweeds made their home. In the midst of these plants, Rosebud, a beautiful pink wild rose, and Daisy Mae, a wild daisy, grew. Daisy Mae and Rosebud were best friends.” Taken from “Daisy Mae and Rosebud”.

I have always loved flowers, especially wildflowers. Wildflowers that grow in ditches, grasses and woods. Hence, this story. I have occasionally wondered if plants, flowers in particular, have a secret life when the sun goes down and are not seen by humans. Do they communicate with each other? I have seen them wave at on another. So… Imagining is one of the very best things a child can do and this beautifully illustrated children’s picture book certainly encourages that. Enjoy!
